Reviewer #1 ->

1. Strength and Weakness of the Contribution

Strengths:

Novel Metric Integration: The shift from traditional SNR-based metrics to detection probability (P_d) as a primary optimization variable is a significant improvement that aligns more closely with real-world sensing requirements.

Multi-Algorithm Benchmarking: The study provides a rigorous comparison across multiple meta-heuristic solvers (GWO, PSO, DE, etc.), offering a transparent view of solver behavior in a complex design space.

Realistic Modeling: The use of the UMi_NLOS scenario and time-varying channel snapshots adds practical depth that is often missing from purely theoretical ISAC papers.

Pareto Analysis: The inclusion of a Pareto frontier demonstrates the tangible benefits of waveform co-optimization over traditional power-only allocation methods like water-filling.

Weaknesses:

Statistical Robustness: The results rely on a fixed-seed setup, which, as the authors admit, limits the statistical significance of the rankings between high-performing solvers like GWO and PSO.

Simplified Sensing Model: The exclusion of clutter, multi-target interference, and noise-clutter covariance limits the immediate applicability of the results to advanced radar receiver designs.

Complexity vs. Latency: While meta-heuristics perform well, the paper lacks a deep discussion on the practical implementation of these high-evaluation-count algorithms in ultra-low-latency 6G environments.


2. Contribution

The article contributes a weighted rate-detection formulation for OFDM-based Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC). Key contributions include:
An expanded design space that treats waveform shaping as a direct variable alongside power allocation.
A reproducible benchmark of optimization behaviors on a realistic 3GPP TR 38.901 motivated channel.
Empirical evidence that detection-aware waveform co-design can nearly double detection probability at specific rate-focused operating points compared to traditional water-filling.


3. Significance of Topic, Presentation, Technical Contribution and Originality

Significance of Topic: High. ISAC is a cornerstone of 6G research, and optimizing the sensing-communication tradeoff is a critical industry challenge.
Presentation: Excellent. The paper is well-structured, the mathematical modeling (Eq. 1-6) is clear, and the graphical representations of the Pareto frontier and waveform profiles are highly informative.

Technical Contribution: Substantial. The joint co-design of power and normalized waveform profiles using detection probability offers a more nuanced control mechanism than standard SNR proxies.

Originality: Moderate-to-High. While waveform shaping in ISAC is not new, the specific application of meta-heuristic comparison driven by P_d in a dynamic UMi_NLOS setting is a fresh perspective.


4. Overall Comments
This is a high-quality submission that effectively bridges the gap between signal processing theory and optimization meta-heuristics. The "spectrum emphasis" interpretation of the waveform profile provides a clear physical intuition for the mathematical results. The authors are commendably transparent about their modeling simplifications and the limitations of their statistical scope. The paper successfully proves its thesis: waveform co-optimization provides a significantly more useful ISAC tradeoff than power-only allocation.



#Reviewer 2 ->

1. Strength and Weakness of the Contribution

The paper addresses an important and emerging problem in Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC) systems by focusing on detection probability instead of conventional sensing SNR metrics.
The study introduces joint power and waveform co-optimization, which expands the design space beyond traditional power-only allocation approaches.
The work provides a detailed comparative benchmark of multiple optimization algorithms including GWO, PSO, DE, SA, SFO, POA, and NSGA-II under the same experimental conditions.
The inclusion of Pareto analysis effectively demonstrates the sensing–communication tradeoff and highlights the practical benefits of waveform co-optimization.
The manuscript clearly presents system assumptions, optimization objectives, mathematical formulations, and reproducibility settings, improving transparency and research clarity.

The sensing model is highly simplified, considering only a single point target without clutter, interference, or multi-target effects, which limits real-world applicability.
The experiments are conducted using fixed random seeds and limited snapshots, reducing the statistical significance and generalizability of the reported results.
The work mainly evaluates optimization behavior and does not incorporate practical detector-side implementations such as matched filtering or MMSE-based receiver processing.
The manuscript occasionally contains repetitive explanations and lengthy descriptive discussions that could be made more concise and technically focused.
The paper lacks comparison with recent deep learning or reinforcement learning based ISAC optimization approaches, which could strengthen the benchmarking study.


2. Contribution

The paper proposes a detection-probability-driven ISAC resource allocation framework with joint power and waveform co-design.
It formulates a weighted sensing–communication optimization problem using detection probability as the sensing metric.
The study introduces waveform shaping as an explicit optimization variable for balancing sensing and communication performance.
The work benchmarks multiple metaheuristic optimization algorithms in a realistic UMi_NLOS OFDM environment.
The research demonstrates that waveform co-optimization significantly improves the sensing–communication Pareto tradeoff compared to traditional power-only methods such as water-filling.


3. Significance of Topic, Presentation, Technical Contribution and Originality

The topic is highly significant for future 5G/6G wireless systems where ISAC is expected to play a major role in simultaneous communication and sensing applications.
The technical contribution is meaningful because it shifts the sensing objective from indirect SNR-based metrics to detection probability, which is more practically relevant for detection-oriented systems.
The inclusion of waveform shaping together with power allocation adds originality and improves the flexibility of ISAC resource optimization.
The paper presents comprehensive mathematical modeling, optimization settings, and evaluation procedures, making the study reproducible and technically sound.
Although the presentation is generally clear, some sections could be shortened and refined to improve readability and reduce repetition.


4. Overall Comments

The paper presents a focused and technically relevant study on detection-aware resource allocation in ISAC systems.
The proposed joint power and waveform co-optimization framework provides meaningful insights into sensing–communication tradeoffs.
The comparative evaluation of multiple optimization techniques strengthens the contribution and offers useful benchmarking results for future ISAC research.
The work would benefit from more realistic sensing models, statistical validation across multiple runs, and inclusion of practical detector-side baselines.
Overall, the paper demonstrates good technical depth, clear experimental methodology, and promising research contribution, making it a valuable study with scope for further extension and refinement.




#Reviewer 3 ->

1. Strength and Weakness of the Contribution

Strengths:

Advanced Metric Shift: Replaces the standard but limited SNR-based sensing metric with Detection Probability ($P_d$), making the sensing objective more physically meaningful for real-world radar applications.

Joint Optimization: Successfully co-designs power allocation and waveform shaping, treating the waveform profile as a direct design variable rather than an afterthought.

Effective Trade-off Management: Uses the Grey Wolf Optimizer (GWO) to map a clear Pareto front, allowing for precise selection between sensing-focused and communication-focused performance.

Superior Performance over Baselines: Demonstrates that the proposed method can double detection probability compared to traditional "Water-filling" algorithms while maintaining nearly the same communication rate.

Scenario-Specific Rigor: Validates the model using a standardized UMi_NLOS (Urban Micro Non-Line-of-Sight) scenario, ensuring the results are relevant to 5G/6G channel modeling.

Weaknesses:

Heuristic Dependency: The reliance on Grey Wolf Optimizer (GWO) means the solution is meta-heuristic; it may require multiple runs to ensure global convergence compared to convex optimization.
Computational Complexity: Jointly optimizing power and waveform across multiple subcarriers is computationally more demanding than power-only allocation.

Fixed False-Alarm Rate: The study fixes the False-Alarm Probability ($P_{fa}$) at $10^{-3}$; performance characteristics might shift significantly if the $P_{fa}$ requirements are tightened for high-security applications.

Limited Hardware Context: While the theory is sound, the paper does not deeply explore the hardware constraints of the transmitter, such as the Peak-to-Average Power Ratio (PAPR) of the co-designed waveforms.

Environmental Sensitivity: The results are highly dependent on the specific NLOS channel parameters; performance in Line-of-Sight (LOS) or high-mobility scenarios is not extensively detailed.


2. Contribution

Detection-Aware Formulation: Provides a new mathematical framework for ISAC (Integrated Sensing and Communication) that prioritizes the probability of detecting a target over mere signal strength.

Waveform Profile Variable: Contributes a "normalized waveform profile" variable that allows for subcarrier-level tuning of the sensing signal.

Pareto Trade-off Analysis: Offers an empirical mapping of the tension between Spectral Efficiency (bps/Hz) and Detection Probability, providing a decision-making tool for network operators.

Comparison to Water-filling: Contributes a technical proof that traditional communication-centric power allocation (Water-filling) is significantly suboptimal for integrated sensing tasks.

ISAC Resource Blueprint: Provides a reusable algorithmic structure for resource allocation in time-varying OFDM channels.


3. Significance of Topic, Presentation, Technical Contribution and Originality

Significance of Topic: ISAC is a cornerstone of 6G technology, turning communication networks into giant sensors for traffic monitoring, gesture recognition, and environmental mapping.

Quality of Presentation: The paper effectively utilizes Pareto curves and performance tables to illustrate the "sensing-communication" conflict, making technical trade-offs easy to visualize.

Technical Contribution: The co-design of power and waveform—rather than optimizing them sequentially—represents a sophisticated technical leap in signal processing.

Originality of Metric: Using $P_d$ as a direct optimization objective in a weighted-rate formulation is an original departure from the "SNR-proxy" approach common in many ISAC papers.

Optimization Versatility: The adaptation of the Grey Wolf Optimizer for this specific multi-objective ISAC problem shows original application of bio-inspired algorithms to telecommunications.


4. Overall Comments

Highly Relevant for 6G: This research directly addresses one of the primary challenges of future networks: how to share a limited spectrum between two competing functions without failing at either.

Validated Efficiency: The fact that the model can double $P_d$ at the rate-focused end proves that current allocation standards are leaving significant "sensing performance" on the table.

Practical Optimization Strategy: By using GWO, the researchers offer a pathway to solve non-convex ISAC problems that are typically difficult for standard solvers.

Balanced Design: The paper excels at maintaining a "dual-purpose" mindset, ensuring that the communication rate ($22.76~bps/Hz$) remains competitive even when sensing is introduced.

Strong Foundation for Future Work: This work paves the way for adding more variables to the co-design, such as beamforming or multi-user interference management.
